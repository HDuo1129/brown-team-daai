## Overview

This project used AI assistance at three distinct stages: data collection and engineering (Session 1), news classification and expectations panel construction (Session 2), and econometric analysis and reporting (Session 3). Below we document what was delegated to AI, what we verified manually, and where human judgment governed the final decisions.

---

## 1. Code Generation (Claude Sonnet via Claude Code CLI)

We used **Claude Code** (Anthropic's CLI tool, powered by `claude-sonnet-4-6`) throughout the project as a coding assistant. Interactions were conversational and iterative — we described what we wanted, reviewed the generated code, tested it, and revised the prompt when output was incorrect.

### Session 1 — Data Collection & Feature Engineering

| Task | AI Role | Human Verification |
|------|---------|--------------------|
| Transfermarkt scraping (`scrape_managers.py`, `scrape_manager_profiles.py`) | Generated boilerplate; rate-limiting and anti-scrape headers suggested by AI | Manually checked 50+ stints against Transfermarkt pages; fixed club name mismatches by hand |
| Club name mapping (`team_mapping.csv`) | AI drafted candidate mappings | All 65 entries reviewed manually; 12 ambiguous cases resolved by human research |
| Feature engineering (`team_home_away.csv`, `team_location.csv`) | Schema design and pandas code drafted by AI | Reviewed aggregation logic; confirmed regional classifications against Turkish geography |
| Data quality tests (`tests/test_data.py`) | Test stubs generated by AI | Added domain-specific checks (e.g. stint date ordering, FTR value range) ourselves |

### Session 2 — News Collection & Classification

| Task | AI Role | Human Verification |
|------|---------|--------------------|
| RSS collector (`news/collect_rss.py`) | Query strings and deduplication logic drafted by AI | Tested against live feeds; manually checked for duplicate or irrelevant articles |
| Manager filter (`news/filter_manager_articles.py`) | Regex patterns for Turkish manager keywords drafted by AI | Reviewed false-positive and false-negative cases; adjusted thresholds |
| Aggregation script (`news/build_expectations.py`) | Pandas groupby logic drafted by AI | Verified output counts against raw article counts |

### Session 3 — Panel Construction & Analysis

| Task | AI Role | Human Verification |
|------|---------|--------------------|
| Panel builder (`analysis/build_panel_full.py`) | Join logic and event-time calculation drafted by AI | Checked event counts and ±10 window logic manually |
| DiD analysis (`analysis/did_analysis.py`) | pyfixest feols calls and model specifications drafted by AI | Reviewed coefficient signs, standard errors, and R² against economic priors |
| `expected_change` threshold decision | AI flagged the THRESHOLD=0.5 problem; presented median-split option | **Human decision**: chose median split over collecting additional data |
| Report (`report.qmd`) | Document structure, LaTeX equations, Quarto callouts drafted by AI | All interpretation, limitation discussion, and conclusion text written or approved by team |

## 2. LLM-Based Article Classification (Claude Haiku via Anthropic API)

We used **`claude-haiku-4-5-20251001`** to classify 2,524 manager-relevant news articles on a 0–4 expectation pressure scale. This is the core AI-in-the-loop component of the research design.

### Prompt Engineering

The classification prompt (`news/prompt_classifier.md`) was developed iteratively:

1. **v1 (initial):** Basic 0–4 scale with minimal examples → failed on appointment articles (scored 4 instead of 0)
2. **v2 (revised):** Added explicit rule: "appointment announcements score 0 — they describe the post-change period" → resolved appointment scoring
3. **v3 (final):** Added JSON output format requirement and `is_relevant` flag → eliminated parse failures

Full prompt: [`news/prompt_classifier.md`](news/prompt_classifier.md)

### Validation Against Human Labels

Before scaling to all 2,524 articles, we validated against 10 hand-labelled articles spanning the full score range:

| Metric | Result | Threshold |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Score agreement (±1 tolerance) | ≥ 90% | ≥ 80% required |
| `is_relevant` agreement | ≥ 80% | ≥ 80% required |

Validation details: [`news/validation_interpretation.md`](news/validation_interpretation.md)

### Known Limitations of AI Classification

- **Headlines only for 96% of articles**: Google News GDPR redirects blocked full-text retrieval; Haiku classified most articles based on title alone. This likely adds noise to scores 1–3 (moderate signal is harder to detect from headlines).
- **Turkish language**: Most articles are in Turkish. Haiku handles Turkish reasonably well, but nuanced sarcasm or idiom may be misread.
- **Score 4 over-representation (21.6%)**: Many articles written *after* a confirmed firing reference it. The `is_relevant` flag and 1-week lag in the analysis panel partially mitigate this.

## 3. What AI Did NOT Do

The following decisions were made entirely by the team without AI delegation:

- **Research question** and causal identification strategy
- **Treatment definition** (mid-season change, caretaker exclusion, minimum pre-period rule)
- **Choice of ±10 match event window**
- **Missing data policy** for pre-RSS seasons (NaN, not imputed)
- **Econometric specification** selection (team-season FE + season-week FE + opponent FE)
- **Interpretation of results** — including the mean reversion pre-trend as the central threat to identification
- **Decision not to collect 2024-25 news data** (time constraints, exploratory scope)
- **All text in the final report** (Introduction, Identification, Discussion, Conclusion sections)

## 4. Reflections on AI-Assisted Research

Using AI as a coding assistant accelerated data pipeline development substantially. Tasks that would have taken hours of boilerplate writing (scraper setup, merge logic, figure generation) were completed in minutes. However, the AI made frequent errors that required domain knowledge to catch:

- **Wrong join keys**: Early versions of the panel builder joined on calendar date rather than the correct manager stint overlap logic.
- **THRESHOLD=0.5 failure**: The initial `expected_change` specification left only 1 of 10 treated observations as "expected", which the AI did not flag proactively — we caught it by inspecting the output.
- **`.style` accessor bug**: AI-generated table styling code failed silently in the Quarto environment; required manual debugging.

These cases illustrate that AI assistance is most effective when the researcher retains enough domain understanding to evaluate output critically, not just accept it.
